About KOLT Python

KOLT Python is founded by Ahmet Uysal in collaboration with Koç University Office of Learning and Teaching (KOLT) and Office of the Vice President for Academic Affairs (VPAA) in September 2018 to address students' rising demand for programming. The program is designed for students with no programming experience and assumes no technical background. In parallel with KOLT’s mission, we value active student engagement and try to create an environment where both the learner and the peer as an instructor can actively engage with the content and each other. We prioritize practical use cases of programming over more theoretical aspects and encourage students to leverage their programming skills in their field or interests during and after the program.

Our Goals

  • Provide a safe learning environment for students from every background to gain their first programming experience.
  • Teach practical programming skills to interested students where each student can further excel and leverage their newly gained programming skills in their field or interests.
  • Establish a unique environment for Koç students who are interested in teaching where they can experience every aspect of teaching including giving lectures to crowded audiences and improve their teaching skills utilizing peer-support.
  • Create high quality and open source educational content.

About Certificate Program

KOLT Python Certificate program is a 12 week long journey that aims to provide students with an understanding of the role programming can play in solving problems and to help students, regardless of their major, rely on their ability to write small programs that allow them to accomplish social, civic, professional and personal valuable goals. To reach more detailed information about the program, please view the syllabus.

Student Reviews

İrem Sot, PhD Candidate in Design, Technology and Society, Fall 2019
As a person who caught up in Social Sciences for 10 years, I had several reservations before starting the KOLT Python Program. Apart from lacking any sort of knowledge about computer programming, the mere text-based education that I have obtained in the past years led me to question whether I would have been capable of learning the programming or not. Nevertheless, both the design of the program which consists of lectures on Mondays and the exercise sessions with 12 students, and our trainers who allocate their spare time to our learning and employ rather simplistic and easy-to-grasp language made me realize that my thoughts regarding Python were just prejudice. Furthermore, the program is very lucrative in adopting participant oriented approach; the entire lecture in the Lab is projected on 4 screens, each lecture and solutions to the problems are promptly made available online and 4 of our lecturers are at our disposal either throughout the class or email. This participant-centered approach surely facilitates asking the questions that most of us label as “embarrassing”.
